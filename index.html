Waxify - My Vinyl Collection Digitized

Click the picture to see more!

I've been collecting vinyl records since high school. I love music, and I love the physical media of vinyl! Waxify first started as an idea to create a virtual record player, but not one of high fidelity. I wanted that looked hand drawn, and simple. And so, I drew each aspect of the record player, uploaded the images and learned CSS animations as best I could! Once that was complete I saw that as a point of entry for my entire collection. The rest is history. This Full Stack Application was built with Vue.js, C#, DotNet, and MySQL. I'm proud with how it turned out, and I hope you enjoy! Click here to learn more about the process!

Keepr - Photo Sharing App (Unhosted)

As part of my certification course, the final project was to build an app in which users could post, keep, and share pictures. Essentially a Pinterest clone. Coding this app took at least two weeks worth of headaches, second guessing myself, and Red Bull. Lots of Red Bull. The hardest part for me was locking things down so users with malicious intent couldn't mess with other people's accounts. The whole backend was a nightmare for me because of that reason. Much like Waxify, this full stack app was built with Vue.js, C#, DotNet, and MySQL. I'm proud of the work I did, and I hope you like it too!

The Nonchalant Perfectionist Podcast

I've been podcasting for close to 5 years, and I love it. At first, I started the podcast to document my journey of a young, struggling entrepreneur. As that journey progressed, I learned that in order to succeed as a business I needed to be in a better place mentally and emotionally. And so, I pivoted to discussing the immense topic of emotional resilience.

After publishing my book, "Nonchalant Perfectionism", I used my podcast to interview what I call Freaking Real, Everyday Dudes about their life and their story. I find it uplifting to learn about regular people, and seeing what keeps them going. How their mind works, and what they do to improve themselves.

Nonchalant Perfectionism:

Man's Guide to Developing Emotional Resilience

Truthfully, I never ever planned to write a book. Mainly because I don't read regularly, and I have never read books regularly. Matter of fact, I was so good at not reading that I never once read an assigned book in school.

Yet, after I attempted to take my life in the summer of 2021, I knew that I had to do something to help myself heal. And so, selfishly, I took to writing and documenting my process of growth. And so, in November 2023 I self-published my journey to a healthy level of emotional stability.
